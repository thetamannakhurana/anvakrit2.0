About NFSU

The National Forensic Sciences University (NFSU), an Institution of National Importance under the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, is the world's first university dedicated exclusively to forensic, behavioral, cybersecurity, digital, and allied sciences. Established to strengthen the criminal justice system through scientific investigation, NFSU combines research, innovation, and academic excellence to prepare professionals contributing to justice and national security.

The Delhi Campus of NFSU serves as a leading centre for education and research in forensic and allied sciences. Located in the national capital, it bridges academia with law enforcement and judicial practice, offering state-of-the-art facilities, expert faculty, and hands-on learning to nurture future forensic professionals.

About The Event

Beyond the Crime Scene

After the resounding success of its first edition, Anvakrit 2.0 returns with greater depth and tougher challenges.

This year, the competition extends across all domains of forensic expertise, including forensic science, criminology, forensic accounting, and law, with a special emphasis on cybercrime and digital forensics. The theme, “Unmask the Truth in Bytes,” invites participants to tackle complex investigations that go beyond the traditional crime scene, venturing into the fast-evolving world where every clue traced physically or digitally strings together the pieces of a puzzle which shall unfurl the truth.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers

What is Anvakrit 2.0? ▶

Anvakrit 2.0 is the second edition of the National Crime Scene Investigation Competition (NCSIC), organized by the National Forensic Sciences University, Delhi Campus. It provides hands-on experience in forensic investigation, bridging theory and practice through simulated crime scenes, digital forensics, and courtroom presentations.

When and where will Anvakrit 2.0 be held? ▶

The event will take place from 11th to 13th February 2026 at the National Forensic Sciences University, Delhi Campus.

What is the theme for this year's edition? ▶

The theme for Anvakrit 2.0 is "Decoding the Truth in Bytes." It focuses on Digital Forensics, encouraging participants to explore the intersection of physical and digital evidence in modern investigations.

Who can participate? ▶

The competition is open to:

  • Category I (UG & Higher Secondary): Students of Grades 11, 12, and undergraduate programs across all streams.
  • Category II (PG & Above): Postgraduate, doctoral, and professional participants from criminology, forensic science, law, psychology, cyber/digital forensics, and allied fields.
Do I need prior forensic knowledge to participate? ▶

No prior forensic knowledge is required for Category I participants. The event is designed to foster curiosity, teamwork, and creative problem-solving while introducing participants to the basics of forensic investigation.

How are the rounds structured for Category I (UG & Higher Secondary)? ▶

Category I includes three rounds:

  1. Case File Investigation – Analytical problem-solving through a given case file.
  2. Poster Presentation – Creative display of findings and methodologies.
  3. On-Campus Crime Scene – Practical investigation challenge and lab visits.
What is the internet/device policy during online rounds? ▶
  • Participants must ensure a stable internet connection and personal device with camera access (if required).
  • Any malpractice, unauthorized device use, or multiple-login attempts will lead to disqualification.
  • Teams must work independently—no external assistance or shared screens beyond their group is allowed.
How will we receive the links for Round 1 and Round 2 for Category 1? ▶

All registered participants will receive official communication via email or the Unstop dashboard.

Links and submission portals will be shared at least 24 hours before each round. Participants should check their registered email regularly and follow all submission deadlines strictly.

What is the policy on plagiarism, intellectual property, or AI-tool usage? ▶
  • All submissions (case answers, posters, and reports) must be original and maintain academic integrity.
  • A maximum of 12% similarity (as per standard plagiarism detection tools) is permitted. Anything beyond that will lead to penalty or disqualification based on the severity.
  • The use of AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Canva AI, Copilot, etc.) is strictly regulated — they may be used only for design enhancement or grammar refinement, not for generating content or analysis.
  • Participants must declare if AI assistance was used in any form. Unacknowledged use or misrepresentation will result in disqualification.
  • The Organizing Committee reserves IP rights over all materials produced or displayed during the event for academic and promotional use.
Who should I contact for technical issues during online rounds? ▶

For any technical problems (login, link access, submission errors), participants can reach out to the Anvakrit 2.0 Technical Support Team through:

  • Email: csi_dc@nfsu.ac.in
  • Helpline: Tamanna Khurana (+91 8881666088)

All issues should be reported immediately during the round window; late complaints will not be considered.

How are the rounds structured for Category II (PG & Professionals)? ▶

Category II includes:

  1. Case File Investigation – Analytical problem-solving based on an assigned case.
  2. On-Campus Crime Scene – Multi-layered forensic investigation.
  3. Mock Courtroom Presentation – Defending findings before a live jury.
How do I register for Anvakrit 2.0? ▶

Registration will be conducted on a first-come, first-serve basis.

  • UG Category: Teams of 4 members must register through Unstop
  • PG Category: Participants may register individually or as a team of 5 via Google Form.
What are the registration fees? ▶
  • Category I (UG & Higher Secondary): ₹1000/- per head
  • Category II (PG & Professionals): ₹2000/- per head

Each participant must register individually.

Is the registration fee refundable? ▶

The registration fee is non-refundable under all circumstances.

What is the last date for registration? ▶

The last date to register is 15th December 2025. Confirmation will be sent after verification by the Organizing Committee.

Can I change my team members after registration? ▶

No, changes in team members or composition will not be allowed after registration. Any failure to adhere to the registration process will be treated as withdrawal.

What are the prizes? ▶

Prizes worth INR 1.2 lakh will be awarded to the Top 3 teams across categories. All participants will receive certificates of participation.

PG participants eliminated before the finals may volunteer for the rest of the event and receive a "Guest Volunteer" Certificate.

What should participants bring? ▶
  • Institutional/School ID card
  • Personal stationery
  • At least one laptop per team for documentation
  • Comfortable formal attire for investigation and courtroom sessions

Essential forensic kits and materials will be provided by the organizers.

Will accommodation be provided? ▶

Yes, accommodation is available at ₹500 per day. Details regarding location, facilities, and timings will be shared after registration. Participants must follow the accommodation rules and timings strictly.

What is the dress code for the competition? ▶

Participants must wear formal attire (Indian or Western). Since the competition involves physical activity at the crime scene, comfortable formal clothing is recommended.

What is expected of participants during the competition? ▶

Participants must maintain professionalism, discipline, and respect toward organizers, judges, and peers. Any malpractice or disruptive behavior will lead to disqualification.

Grounds for disqualification include:

  • Tampering with or mishandling evidence.
  • Disrupting other teams or violating instructions.
  • Using unauthorized materials or devices.
  • Displaying disrespectful behavior toward organizers, volunteers, or peers.
  • Breach of dress-code or reporting requirements.
How will participants be evaluated? ▶

Evaluation is based on:

  • Speed and accuracy (Case File Investigation)
  • Creativity and presentation (Poster Round)
  • Observation, documentation, and teamwork (Final CSI Challenge)
  • Technical and practical knowledge (Courtroom Presentation – PG Category)
What makes Anvakrit 2.0 unique? ▶

Anvakrit 2.0 combines learning, investigation, and competition—offering real-world forensic exposure, surprise puzzles from registration onwards, and interdisciplinary collaboration that goes beyond the crime scene.

Will there be any surprises or hidden challenges? ▶

Yes! The event begins even before you realize it. From the registration phase, participants may receive clues or puzzles that can give them an edge in the competition—because in Anvakrit, "Every Detail Counts."
